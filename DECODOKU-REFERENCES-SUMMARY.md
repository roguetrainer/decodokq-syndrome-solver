- Extends the concept to different quantum codes

### 2. Differences

We explain how Decodok-Q differs from original Decodoku:

| Aspect | Original Decodoku | Decodok-Q |
|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| Codes | Qudit surface codes (d=10) | Steane & Reed-Muller (d=2) |
| Focus | Research contribution | Education & visualization |
| Platform | Mobile apps | Jupyter notebooks |
| Geometry | 2D lattice | Fano plane & Tetrahedron |

## Name Convention: "Decodok-Q"

Following Decodoku's tradition of replacing "u" with "q" (because it rhymes!), we named this project:

**Decodok-Q** = Decodoku + Q (for Quantum/Qubits)

This playful homage is explained in ORIGINAL-DECODOKU-GAME.md.

## Educational Philosophy Alignment

We align with Wootton's philosophy that:
- Quantum computing should be accessible through games
- The public can contribute to quantum research
- Games provide intuition that complements math
- Education should be playful and engaging
